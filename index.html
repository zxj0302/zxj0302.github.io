Xiangju Zhu

PhD Candidate in Computer Science, The University of Hong Kong. Advised by Prof. Reynold Cheng.

Department of Computer Science

The University of Hong Kong

Pokfulam, Hong Kong SAR

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Hong Kong (HKU), advised by Prof. Reynold Cheng. My research focuses on graph mining and network analysis, with particular interest in:

  • Dense subgraph discovery — scalable algorithms for finding dense structures in large graphs, including variants with negative weights
  • Echo chambers in social networks — detection, measurement, and mitigation of polarization phenomena
  • Multi-attribute shortest path queries — index structures for preference-weighted queries on road networks

Before joining HKU, I received my B.Eng. in Computer Science from Tianjin University (CGPA: 92/100) in 2023, where I was honored as an Outstanding Graduate. I was awarded the National Scholarship twice (2020, 2022) during my undergraduate studies.

I am currently supported by the HKU Postgraduate Scholarship (2023–2027).
